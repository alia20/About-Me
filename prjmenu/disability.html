The use of computers for people who are deaf is very complicated and tough to deal with. Everyday tasks can be very difficult, but assistive technologies are available for them. In most cases, computers can be used to perform any tasks without having to listen to any sounds. Examples for this sending e-mail and browsing the internet. However, the computer responds to actions with sounds to alert us to certain events. So an alternative way is needed for deaf people to alert them to certain events . Also for the hearing-impaired people captioning and transcripts should be provided.

blindenss

Individuals who are blind cannot access visual material presented on the computer screen or in printed materials. Also, blindness interferes with the ability of watching videos, looking at pictures and surfing the web to do shopping or other things. However individuals who are blind can be assisted by various technologies with every day use of computers. This devices vary in cost from free to expensive like $20,000 for a single device. For certain disabilities, a small software or hardware might do the job, but aside from using a screen reader, blind people also need to rely on developers to have their applications coded in an accessible way.
